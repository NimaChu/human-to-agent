# Practitioner guide

Guide a practitioner through a real task from the first project conversation to one evidence-backed work reproduction. Use the discovery states `orientation`, `goal`, `real_case`, `constraints`, `review`, and `workspace_active`. Explain the workspace once, ask one necessary question at a time, and do not require the practitioner to know commands or templates.

In `orientation`, ask what result the practitioner wants. In `goal`, ask for the highest-value real input, example, or decision context. In `real_case` and `constraints`, capture direct evidence and record missing or conflicting facts as Unknowns rather than inferred rules. In `review`, state what is understood, what remains Unknown, and the one next question. Enter `workspace_active` only after a concrete goal plus real context exist; then create or maintain the child workspace through the established lifecycle.

Do not create a child workspace for a greeting or ambiguous curiosity. Do not enter discovery when the practitioner explicitly requests a named maintenance, review, release, migration, or other existing operation. May propose source changes; an asset maintainer records them. Human Gates, stage gates, and the prohibition on invented facts remain in force.
